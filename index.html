Two years ago, I launched my first online course from my driveway at home.

×

I started with the platform called New Zenler.

×

We were starting on a shoestring budget.

×

I went through various platforms to choose the best one for me at the time.

×

I initially built my courses in Thinkific but didn't like the site builder.

×

Somehow, I came across New Zenler, which had the price and functions I needed.

×

New Zenler had a reasonable price and the functions I needed at the time.

×

The community features on New Zenler were poor.

×

New Zenler had many bugs, and their app was really bad.

×

The first reason for switching to EasyCourse was the mobile app.

×

The community features in EasyCourse were much better.

×

EasyCourse offered a tiered seat system for selling courses to organizations.

×

The white-label mobile app at EasyCourse was much more affordable.

×

EasyCourse added in-app purchases for Apple and Android phone users.

×

EasyCourse's community features felt like running our own social media site.

×

EasyCourse allowed us to upload images, videos, and files, and create polls.

×

The tiered seat system allowed organizations to manage their employees' access.

×

We could administrate the seats from the back end with EasyCourse.

×

EasyCourse's support team provided rapid bug fixes.

×

EasyCourse was open to new features and improvements.

×

Upcoming features include a TV app, AI chatbot, and automated payouts for affiliates.
